# Soft-TPM policy synthesizer

Fleet measured-boot needs a local Soft-TPM lab that synthesizes one policy blob authorizing every disclosed boot profile under the published size and NV-index budgets. Fix sources under `/app/environment` so the normal driver regenerates `/app/output/pol_blob.bin` and `/app/output/settle_view.json`. Module behavior must be corrected; static or hand-copied blobs at the output path are insufficient.

The verifier rebuilds via `bash /app/environment/tools/mk_all.sh`, then runs `/app/bin/lab` through the staged `seat`, `weave`, and `seal` path (or `synth`) plus Soft-TPM helpers. Multi-stage argv, generation snapshots, recover, and replay live in `/app/environment/docs/ops.md`. Field rules, epoch-bound NV tokens, phase journal shape, and lineage live in `/app/environment/docs/out_shape.md`. Contract notes live in `/app/environment/docs/lab_contract.md`. Assets: PCR banks under `/app/environment/data/banks/`, profiles under `/app/environment/data/profiles/`, budgets in `/app/environment/data/lim/budgets.toml`, NV seed at `/app/environment/data/nv/counter_seed.bin`.

Write `/app/output/pol_blob.bin` and `/app/output/settle_view.json` through the synthesizer pipeline. settle_view must carry arms, byte_len, view_digest, nv_token, bank_tag, epoch, arms_digest, sync_label, phase_digest, and lineage_hex as defined in out_shape.md. Soft-TPM unseal must succeed for every disclosed profile under `/app/environment/data/profiles/` (including held-out fixtures) from the same regenerated blob. Session-start success does not replace profile unseal success.

Hand-copied goldens are insufficient. Deleting settle_view and replaying from the generation snapshot must restore an equivalent view_digest for the pinned epoch. A stub at the settle_view path, including `/app/environment/data/stub_trace.json`, must be replaced by a freshly settled report. NV binding must survive Soft-TPM reboot, and recover must restore live NV from generation/durable state after damage. The staged `seat`, `weave`, and `seal` path must stay coherent across arm-list changes.
